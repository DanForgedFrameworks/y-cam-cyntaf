Orientation

This short orientation sits alongside Session 1. It is here to lower the pressure and show you how everything works before you step into the room.

Welcome video from Matt (coming soon)

There will be a short video here from Matt, the founder of Tidy Butt. He will welcome you to Y Cam Cyntaf, explain why the course exists, and reassure you that this is about connection, not tests or grades.

What Y Cam Cyntaf is (and is not)

  • A gentle first step into Welsh, with friendly support from Tidy Butt and the group.
  • A space to listen, notice, try a few words, and celebrate small wins together.
  • Not an exam course or a perfect grammar class. Mistakes are expected and welcome.

How learning will happen

  • Short digital “Get Ready” activities between sessions.
  • Low-pressure live sessions every fortnight, with games, stories, and wellbeing check-ins.
  • Light social “homework”, small actions you can try in day-to-day life if you have the energy.

Pressure, mistakes, and participation

  • You choose how much you join in on any given day.
  • You can listen quietly, speak when you feel ready, or just try one word.
  • Mistakes are treated as part of learning, and often part of the laughter.

Digital resources and print-friendly backups

  • Each session has a Get Ready → Live session → After the session structure.
  • You can use this hub on your phone or laptop, or print pages as PDFs using your browser.
  • The Downloads area will grow with handouts, phrase sheets, and curiosity prompts as the course runs.

Curiosity threads

  • Optional “rabbit holes” linked to Welsh nature, place-names, or mythology.
  • There is no expectation to finish them. They are there to spark interest and give you something fun to chat about in Welsh.
  • You will see them labelled as Curiosity thread in the session cards.

When you are ready, head to Session 1 and complete the Get Ready activities. If your time or energy is low, doing just that is more than enough.
