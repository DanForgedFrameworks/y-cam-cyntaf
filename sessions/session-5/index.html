Session 5: Shopping and the Grounded café experience

How this session works

Rhythm: Digital pre-work → Live session → Light homework or social application

This is designed to feel low pressure. Small steps count.

Aim: Try Welsh in a simple café or social transaction without pressure.

How we’ll achieve this: A few useful words, a friendly roleplay, and real-world practice that can be as small as one word.

What we’ll cover: Coffee, tea, thanks, and a simple ordering phrase.

Outcome (plain English): You can try Welsh in a real place, gently and briefly.

How to use this page: Use the arrows to move through the steps. Low time or low energy? Do Get Ready and you will still be ready enough.

Step 1: Get Ready

Try one phrase:

  • Ga i goffi… (Can I have a coffee…)
  • Ga i de… (Can I have a tea…)
  • Diolch (Thank you)

Pick one. That is enough.

Step 2: Get Ready (optional)

Mini practice: Choose a safe version you can actually use.

  • Diolch
  • Shwmae
  • Ga i goffi…

You can keep it to one word if that is all you have today.

Step 3: Live session

  • Pop-up café roleplay (low pressure)
  • Simple greetings and ordering patterns
  • Mentimeter: best biscuit poll

You can watch and listen if you prefer.

Step 4: After the session

Small follow-on: Use any Welsh at Grounded or a café (even one word).

Optional: note the word you tried, privately if you prefer.

Step 5: Downloads

Session handouts will appear as we build them.

Step 6: Curiosity thread (optional)

Choose one:

  • Look up a Welsh food word you like.
  • Notice Welsh in cafés or shops.
  • Bring one word you noticed to the next session.

This is a stretch option. It is for interest and conversation, not performance.
