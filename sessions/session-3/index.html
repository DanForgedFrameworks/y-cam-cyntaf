Session 3: Empathy and Wellness

How this session works

Rhythm: Digital pre-work → Live session → Light homework or social application

This is designed to feel low pressure. Small steps count.

Session aim: Use simple Welsh to check in with yourself and others in a supportive way.

How we’ll do this: A short wellbeing check-in, gentle repetition, and optional sharing in the group.

What we’ll cover: Simple “how are you” phrases and feeling words.

Outcome (plain English): You can express “how you are” in a simple way, and you can recognise it when others use it.

How to use this page: Use Previous and Next to move through the steps. If your energy is low, do Get Ready only.

Step 1: Get Ready

Watch: the “Wellbeing Check-In” video (shared by your facilitator).

Private reflection: “How am I feeling today?” (you do not need to share).

Pick two phrases to try once:

  • Dw i’n iawn (I’m OK)
  • Dw i’n flinedig (I’m tired)
  • Dw i’n hapus (I’m happy)
  • Dw i’n poeni (I’m worried)

If you have a bit more time: add “heddiw” (today), for example “Dw i’n flinedig heddiw”.

Step 2: Live session

  • Supportive check-in patterns.
  • Listening practice (recognising feeling words).
  • Optional pair or group sharing (no pressure).

Step 3: After the session

Social: Ask “Sut wyt ti?” at a Wellness Walk, Circle, Grounded visit, cold dip, or in the WhatsApp group.

Digital: Write one sentence about how you are today (keep it private if you prefer).

Reassurance

You can keep this private. Noticing and trying is the point.

Step 5: Curiosity thread (optional)

Pick one (only if you want to):

  • Find a Welsh word that describes a feeling you like (calm, warm, safe).
  • Look up a Welsh song title and what it means.
  • Learn one Welsh word linked to community or kindness.

This is a stretch option. It is for interest and conversation, not performance.
