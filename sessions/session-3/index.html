Session 3: Empathy and the wellness check-in

How this session works

Rhythm: Digital pre-work → Live session → Light homework or social application

This is designed to feel low pressure. Small steps count.

Aim: Talk about wellbeing in a simple way and check in on someone else.

How we’ll achieve this: Short phrases, gentle listening practice, and a supportive group approach.

What we’ll cover: “Sut wyt ti?” and a few feeling phrases you can choose from.

Outcome (plain English): You can check in with someone kindly, without pressure.

How to use this page: Use the arrows to move through the steps. Low time or low energy? Do Get Ready and you will still be ready enough.

Step 1: Get Ready

Pick two phrases:

  • Dw i’n iawn (I’m okay)
  • Dw i’n flinedig (I’m tired)
  • Dw i’n hapus (I’m happy)
  • Dw i’n poeni (I’m worried)

You do not need to share anything personal. This can be private practice.

Step 2: Get Ready (optional)

Mini build: Add “heddiw” (today).

  • Dw i’n flinedig heddiw
  • Dw i’n iawn heddiw

Only do this if it feels manageable.

Step 3: Live session

  • Mentimeter: anonymous emotional check-in
  • Paired listening and empathy roleplay using “Sut wyt ti?”

You can pass, listen, or keep it light. You are in control.

Step 4: After the session

Small follow-on: Write one sentence about how you are today (keep it private if you prefer).

This is about self-kindness, not perfect Welsh.

Step 6: Curiosity thread (optional)

Choose one:

  • Find a Welsh word that describes a feeling you like (calm, safe, warm).
  • Look up a Welsh song title and what it means.
  • Learn one Welsh word linked to community or kindness.

This is a stretch option. It is for interest and conversation, not performance.
