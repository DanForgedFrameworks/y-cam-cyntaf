Session 3: Talking about yourself, how you are doing, wellbeing

Session aim: Build simple language for checking in and sharing how you are feeling.

How we will achieve this: Gentle, supportive practice with short phrases and optional sharing activities.

What we will cover: Simple feelings words, “I am ok”, “a bit tired”, and friendly check-in responses.

Outcome: You will have a small set of Welsh phrases you can use to check in with others and express yourself safely.

How to use this page: Use the arrows or step buttons. If your time or energy is low, do Get Ready only.

Step 1: Get Ready

Choose one “how are you” phrase from the list your facilitator shares. That is all you need for prep.

Step 2: More Get Ready

Pick one feelings word you like. Write it down. If you want, add an emoji next to it to help you remember.

Step 3: Live session

We will practise check-ins together. You can join in out loud, quietly, or just listen and follow along.

Step 4: After the session

Post a short check-in in WhatsApp. It can be one word. It can be an emoji. It still counts.

Step 5: Curiosity thread

Find a Welsh saying or proverb about wellbeing or nature. Bring it back even if you only remember one word.
