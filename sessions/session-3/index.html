Session 3: Talking about you (and how you are)

Aim: Be able to share a little about yourself and your wellbeing, gently and safely.

How we’ll achieve this: Simple sentence patterns, supportive options, and “you choose how much to share” activities.

What we’ll cover: How you are feeling, basic preferences, short personal statements.

Outcome: You can say a couple of honest, useful sentences in Welsh.

How to use this page: Use the arrows to move through the steps. Low time or energy? Do Get Ready only.

Step 1: Get Ready

Pick two phrases:

  • Dw i’n iawn (I’m OK)
  • Dw i’n flinedig (I’m tired)
  • Dw i’n hapus (I’m happy)
  • Dw i’n poeni (I’m worried)

Step 2: Get Ready (optional)

Mini build: Add “heddiw” (today).

  • Dw i’n flinedig heddiw
  • Dw i’n iawn heddiw

You can keep it simple. Simple is strong.

Step 3: Live session

  • Warm-up chat (low pressure)
  • Sentence patterns for feelings and preferences
  • Supportive group activity: “choose your phrase”

Step 4: After the session

Small follow-on: Write one sentence about how you are today (keep it private if you prefer).
