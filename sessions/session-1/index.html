Session 1: First steps and the social start

Session aim: Get set up, feel welcome, and take your first relaxed step into Welsh.

How we will achieve this: Friendly, low-pressure digital prep, a few playful phrases, and gentle WhatsApp check-ins so you know you are not alone.

What we will cover: Basic hellos, simple “check-in” phrases, platform basics, and how the group works.

Outcome: You will feel confident enough to try a couple of Welsh words, and you will know how to take part in a way that feels comfortable for you.

How to use this page: Use the arrows or step buttons below to move through the cards. If your time or energy is low, just complete Get Ready. That is enough for now.

Step 1: Get Ready

Say “shwmae” to the group.
Join the Y Cam Cyntaf WhatsApp group (your facilitator will share the link). When you are ready, post a single emoji or a simple “shwmae” to say you have arrived.

Step 2: More Get Ready

Listen for a few “silly Welsh” words.
Watch the short video (link provided by your facilitator). Notice how the words sound. If you like, write down any phrases that make you smile and bring them to the live session.

Step 3: Live session

Break the ice together.
You will practise a few friendly phrases, do a simple wellbeing check-in, and take your first steps speaking in a way that feels safe. You choose how much you join in.

Step 4: After the session

Do one tiny follow-up.
Later that day or the next, send one short message to the group. It can be a word you remember, a reaction emoji, or “diolch”. Small wins count.

Step 5: Curiosity thread

A Welsh “rabbit hole”, if you fancy it.
Look up one Welsh place-name near you and see what it means. Bring what you find to share, even if it is just one word.
