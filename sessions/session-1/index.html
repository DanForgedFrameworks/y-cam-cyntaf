Session 1: First steps and the Social start

How this session works

Rhythm: Digital pre-work → Live session → Light homework or social application

This is designed to feel low pressure. Small steps count.

Session aim: Get set up, feel welcome, and take your first relaxed step into Welsh.

How we’ll do this: Light, low-pressure digital prep, a few playful phrases, and gentle WhatsApp check-ins so you know you are not alone.

What we’ll cover: Popti Ping, Hoffi Coffi, platform basics, and saying “Shwmae” in the group.

Outcome (plain English): You will feel confident enough to say a couple of Welsh words and know how to take part in the group in a way that feels comfortable for you.

How to use this page: Use Previous and Next to move through the steps. If your time or energy is low, just complete Get Ready. That is enough for now.

Step 1: Get Ready

Join the WhatsApp group and post one tiny check-in. A single “Shwmae” or one emoji is perfect.

This is about feeling less alone, not about getting it right.

Step 2: Distance Travelled baseline (recommended)

Complete the Distance Travelled confidence check-in before your first live session. It is private, not graded, and you cannot fail.

It helps you notice progress later. If you choose to send your scores, it also helps Tidy Butt show impact for funding and future community delivery.

Open Distance Travelled check-in (opens in new tab)

Step 3: More Get Ready

Watch the “Popti Ping and Hoffi Coffi” video (link from your facilitator).

Notice the sounds. If you want, jot down one phrase you like.

Step 4: Live session

  • Mentimeter word cloud: one word for how you feel about speaking Welsh
  • Group chant of fun phrases to lower anxiety

You can participate quietly. Listening still counts as learning.

Step 5: After the session

Send one tiny reflection: one thing you recognised, one moment you enjoyed, and one thing you want to try again.

Reassurance

This is not a test. You cannot fail. You can listen, notice, and try in your own way.

Step 7: Curiosity thread (optional)

Optional: explore a Welsh place-name or phrase linked to beginnings or paths.

This is a stretch option. It is for interest and conversation, not performance.

  • Bring one place-name or phrase you noticed
  • Optional: a short micro-story or image shared by Tidy Butt
  • Optional: “What does a first step mean to you?” discussion prompt
