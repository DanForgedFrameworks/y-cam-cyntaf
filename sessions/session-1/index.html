Session 1: First steps and the Social start

How this session works

Rhythm: Digital pre-work → Live session → Light homework or social application

This is designed to feel low pressure. Small steps count.

Session aim: Get set up, feel welcome, and take your first relaxed step into Welsh.

How we’ll do this: Light pre-work, a few playful phrases, and gentle WhatsApp check-ins so you know you are not alone.

What we’ll cover: Popti Ping, Hoffi Coffi, platform basics, and saying “shwmae” in the group.

Outcome (plain English): You will feel confident enough to try a couple of Welsh words and take part in the group in a way that suits you.

How to use this page: Use Previous and Next to move through the steps. Low time or low energy? Just complete Get Ready. That is enough for now.

Step 1: Get Ready

  • Join the WhatsApp group (your facilitator will share the link).
  • Post a single emoji or “Shwmae” to say you have arrived.
  • Watch the “Popti Ping & Hoffi Coffi” video (shared by your facilitator).

If you have a bit more time: pick one word or phrase you like from the video and bring it to the session.

Step 2: Quick check-in (optional)

If you have not already done it in Orientation, you can complete the Distance Travelled confidence check-in. It is private, not graded, and you cannot fail.

If you choose to send your scores, it helps Tidy Butt evidence course impact for funding and future community delivery.

Open Distance Travelled check-in (opens in new tab)

Step 3: Live session

  • Warm welcome and group check-in.
  • Playful phrases (including “Shwmae”).
  • Low pressure group participation (listening counts).

Step 4: After the session

Social: Post a voice note or text sharing your favourite funny Welsh word, and reply to one other person.

Digital: Send one tiny reflection (WhatsApp or private): one word you recognised, one moment you enjoyed, or one thing you want to try again.

Reassurance

This is not a test. You cannot fail. You can listen, notice, and try in your own way.

Step 6: Curiosity thread (optional)

Explore one small “first step” curiosity, shared by your facilitator.

  • A Welsh place-name linked to beginnings or paths.
  • A short story, image, or phrase connected to taking a first step.
  • An optional prompt for conversation: “What does a first step mean to you?”

This is a stretch option. It is for interest and conversation, not performance.
