Session 1: Your first steps

Aim: Get comfortable hearing and using a few simple Welsh words and greetings, without pressure.

How we’ll achieve this: Short, friendly practice, lots of repetition, and supportive group activities where it is OK to try, pause, or pass.

What we’ll cover: Basic greetings, saying your name, simple “yes/no”, and a couple of useful “little words”.

Outcome: You leave feeling “I can do this”, and you have a few phrases you can actually use.

How to use this page: Use the arrows to move through the steps for this session. If your time or energy is low, focus on Get Ready only. That is enough to help you (and your group) get more from the live session.

Step 1: Get Ready

5 minutes: Read these out loud once (quietly is fine).

  • Shwmae (hello)
  • Bore da (good morning)
  • Nos da (good night)
  • Diolch (thank you)

Tip: If you feel awkward, that is normal. You are learning a new sound system.

Step 2: Get Ready (optional)

2 minutes: Pick one phrase you want to try in the room.

  • Shwmae, dw i’n … (Hello, I’m …)
  • Dw i’n iawn (I’m OK)
  • Dim yn ddrwg (Not bad)

If you only do Step 1, that is still a win.

Step 3: Live session

In the session we will practise together, gently and at a pace that feels safe.

  • Call-and-response greetings
  • Simple “say your name” practice
  • Low-pressure pair / small group practice (you can always pass)

Step 4: After the session

Small follow-on: Try one greeting once this week (in your head, out loud, or by message).

This is about confidence, not perfection.

Step 6: Curiosity thread (optional)

Pick one tiny curiosity and bring it to the room next time.

  • Find a Welsh place name near you and learn how to say it.
  • Look up one Welsh word connected to nature (river, hill, tree).
  • Ask someone: “Do you know any Welsh words?” (in English is fine).
