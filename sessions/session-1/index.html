Session 1: First steps and the social start

Session aim: Get set up, feel welcome, and take your first relaxed step into Welsh.

How we’ll do this: Light, low-pressure digital prep, a few playful phrases, and gentle WhatsApp check-ins so you know you are not alone.

What we’ll cover: Popti Ping, Hoffi Coffi, platform basics, and saying “shwmae” in the group.

Outcome (in plain English): You’ll feel confident enough to say a couple of Welsh words and know how to take part in the group in a way that feels comfortable for you.

How to use this page: Use the arrows or step buttons to move through the cards below. Aim to try each step if you can. If your time or energy are low, just complete Get Ready – that is enough for now.

Step 1 • Get Ready

Say shwmae to the group

Join the Y Cam Cyntaf WhatsApp group (your facilitator will share the link). When you are ready, post a single emoji or a simple “Shwmae” to say you have arrived.

You do not need to write more than that. This is simply your first tiny step and lets others see that they are not the only new person.

Step 2 • Get Ready

Watch “Popti Ping & Hoffi Coffi”

Watch the short video (link provided by your facilitator). Just notice how the words sound – there is no test.

If you like, write down any phrases that make you smile. You can bring them to the live session to share with the group.

Step 3 • Live session

Break the ice together

In the live session you will take part in light-hearted activities: word clouds, group chants of fun phrases, and a chance to meet the others.

You choose how much you speak out loud – listening quietly is always allowed.

Step 4 • After the session

Share a favourite Welsh word

When you feel ready, send a short message or voice note in the WhatsApp group with a Welsh word you enjoyed (for example, Popti Ping).

Reply to at least one other person to keep the conversation gentle and social.

Curiosity thread

First steps and Welsh place-names

Optional: explore a short story or image about journeys and “first steps” in Welsh. Your facilitator may share a place-name or path that links to starting out.

You can simply look at it, or bring one thought to the next session: What does a first step mean to you?
