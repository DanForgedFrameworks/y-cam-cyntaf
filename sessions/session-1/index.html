Session 1: The Foundation and the Social Start

Session aim: To navigate the platform, join the social community, and break the ice with fun colloquialisms.

Key topics: Popti Ping, Hoffi Coffi, Platform and WhatsApp setup

Bloom’s outcome: Identify key colloquialisms and initiate contact in the social group.

You will achieve this by doing a short “Get Ready”, practising together in the live session, then posting a small follow-up in the social group to make it stick.

Your session steps

How to use this: Use the arrows above, or select a box below. You can move in either direction and it loops around.

Get Ready

Why it helps: a short warm-up, so the live session feels low-pressure.

Digital (pre-work):

  • Action: Join the Tidy Butt Learners WhatsApp group and confirm you have arrived.
  • Fun: Watch the “Popti Ping and Hoffi Coffi” video.

Aim: 10 minutes. Keep it light.

Live session

Why it helps: you practise with people, not on your own, and confidence grows quickly.

Theme: Breaking the Ice (60 to 90 mins)

  • Introductions and a relaxed start
  • Low-stakes speaking practice with fun phrases
  • Short group activity to reduce anxiety and build connection

After session

Why it helps: a tiny follow-up makes next time easier.

Homework (social):

  • Task: Post a voice note or a text in the WhatsApp group introducing yourself.
  • Bonus: Include one “funny” Welsh word (for example: Popti Ping).
  • Connection: Reply to one other person.

Curiosity Thread

Why it helps: it gives you something interesting to share, which supports wellbeing and conversation.

Optional: You can skip this if you are busy.

  • A link or short clip about Welsh culture, nature, or mythology
  • A simple prompt for discussion in the group chat
  • Optional: a quick poll (Mentimeter) or a reflection question

Intent: spark interest and conversation, not learning or recall.
