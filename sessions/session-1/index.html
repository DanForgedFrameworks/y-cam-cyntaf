Session 1: First steps and the social start

Session aim: Get set up, feel welcome, and take your first relaxed step into Welsh.

How we’ll do this: Light, low-pressure digital prep, a few playful phrases, and gentle WhatsApp check-ins so you know you are not alone.

What we’ll cover: Popti Ping, Hoffi Coffi, platform basics, and saying “shwmae” in the group.

Outcome (plain English): You will feel confident enough to say a couple of Welsh words and know how to take part in the group in a way that feels comfortable for you.

How to use this page: Use Previous and Next to move through the steps. If your time or energy is low, just complete Get Ready. That is enough for now.

Step 1: Get Ready

Post one simple check-in in the WhatsApp group. A single “Shwmae” is perfect. This is just to help you feel less alone before the session.

Step 2: Quick check-in (optional)

Have you done the Distance Travelled confidence check-in yet? It is private, not graded, and you cannot fail.

It helps you notice progress later. If you choose to send your scores, it also helps Tidy Butt show impact for funding and future community delivery.

Open Distance Travelled check-in

Step 3: More Get Ready

Watch the short “Popti Ping & Hoffi Coffi” video (link from your facilitator). Notice the sounds. No test. If you want, jot down one phrase you like.

Step 4: Live session

Bring your chosen phrase to the session if you feel up to it. You can participate quietly. Listening still counts as learning.

Step 5: After the session

After the live session, send one tiny reflection: one word you recognised, one moment you enjoyed, or one thing you want to try again.
