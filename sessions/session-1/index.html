Session 1: First steps and the social start

Session aim: Get set up, feel welcome, and take your first relaxed step into Welsh.

How we’ll achieve this: We’ll use friendly, low-pressure practice, a few fun phrases, and a simple WhatsApp check-in so you can connect with others without feeling put on the spot.

What we’ll cover: Popti Ping, Hoffi Coffi, pronunciation basics, how the group works.

Outcome (in plain English): You’ll feel confident enough to say a couple of Welsh words, and you’ll know how to take part in the group in a way that feels comfortable.

How to use this page: Use the arrows to move through the steps. If time or energy is low, do Get Ready only. That is enough.

Session steps

Get Ready

Time: 5 to 10 minutes.

  • Join the WhatsApp group.
  • Post a single emoji or “Bore da” so we know you are in.
  • Watch the short “Popti Ping and Hoffi Coffi” clip.

If you do nothing else, do this. It helps you arrive ready.

Live Session

Time: 60 to 90 minutes.

  • Gentle warm-up and introductions.
  • Try a few fun phrases together, no pressure to perform.
  • Agree WhatsApp norms: kindness, low pressure, reply later is fine.

Focus is confidence and connection, not perfect Welsh.

After Session

Time: 5 minutes.

  • Post one phrase you enjoyed (or a voice note if you feel up to it).
  • Reply to one other person with encouragement.

This keeps the momentum going between sessions.

Curiosity Thread

Optional: 5 to 15 minutes.

  • Explore a Welsh place-name or phrase linked to the local area.
  • Share one interesting thing you noticed in the group.

This is for interest and wellbeing, not homework.
