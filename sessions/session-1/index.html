Session 1: First steps and the social start

Session aim: Get set up, feel welcome, and take your first relaxed step into Welsh.

How we’ll achieve this: We’ll use friendly, low-pressure practice, a few fun phrases (including some silly Welsh terms), and a simple check-in so you can connect with others without feeling put on the spot.

What we’ll cover: silly Welsh terms, basic course setup, group norms, and a gentle first practice.

Outcome: You’ll feel confident enough to try a couple of Welsh words, and you’ll know how to take part in the group in a way that feels comfortable.

How to use this page: Use the arrows to move through the steps for this session. We understand you are doing this in your own time, so engage with the resources as much as you can. They are here to help you and your peers get the most from the live session. If your time or energy is low, do Get Ready only, so everyone arrives ready. Even if you are not all on the same page, you will be in the same book.

Session steps

Get Ready

Time: 5 to 10 minutes.

  • Join the WhatsApp group (from your facilitator).
  • Post a single emoji or “Bore da” so we know you are in.
  • Watch a short clip with a few fun phrases and silly Welsh terms.

If you do nothing else, do this. It helps you arrive ready.

Live Session

Time: 60 to 90 minutes.

  • Gentle warm-up and introductions.
  • Try a few fun phrases together, no pressure to perform.
  • Agree group norms: kindness, low pressure, reply later is fine.

Focus is confidence and connection, not perfect Welsh.

After Session

Time: 5 minutes.

  • Post one phrase you enjoyed (text or voice note if you feel up to it).
  • Reply to one other person with encouragement.

This keeps things warm between sessions.

Curiosity Thread

Optional: 5 to 15 minutes.

  • Explore a Welsh place-name, nature word, or short local story.
  • Share one interesting thing you noticed in the group.

This is for interest and wellbeing, not homework.
