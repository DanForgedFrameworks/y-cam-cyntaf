Session 4: Out and about

Aim: Use Welsh for simple, real-world moments when you are out and about.

How we’ll achieve this: Useful phrases, practical scenarios, and gentle group practice.

What we’ll cover: Simple directions, places, and everyday interactions.

Outcome: You can use Welsh in small “real life” moments without freezing.

How to use this page: Use the arrows to move through the steps. If energy is low, do Get Ready only.

Step 1: Get Ready

Pick two phrases:

  • Ble mae …? (Where is …?)
  • Dw i’n mynd i … (I’m going to …)
  • Yma / Yno (Here / There)

Step 2: Get Ready (optional)

Mini practice: Choose one place and try a full question.

  • Ble mae’r siop? (Where is the shop?)
  • Ble mae’r parc? (Where is the park?)

Step 3: Live session

  • Scenario practice: asking where something is
  • Useful “out and about” vocabulary
  • Supportive group role-play (no pressure)

Step 4: After the session

Small follow-on: On your next walk, name one thing in Welsh (even quietly to yourself).

Step 6: Curiosity thread (optional)

  • Learn one Welsh word for something you notice outside (sky, rain, wind).
  • Look up a local landmark and its Welsh name.
  • Bring one Welsh place word next time (street, river, hill).
