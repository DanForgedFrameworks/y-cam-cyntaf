Session 4: Outside (walks, cold dips and the weather)

How this session works

Rhythm: Digital pre-work → Live session → Light homework or social application

This is designed to feel low pressure. Small steps count.

Aim: Name simple kit and weather words that help you feel safe and prepared outdoors.

How we’ll achieve this: A small set of useful words, matching activities, and real-world noticing.

What we’ll cover: Towel, shoes, clothes, cold, rain, and conditions.

Outcome (plain English): You can describe kit and conditions in a simple way.

How to use this page: Use the arrows to move through the steps. Low time or low energy? Do Get Ready and you will still be ready enough.

Step 1: Get Ready

Try these words once:

  • Tywel (towel)
  • Esgidiau (shoes)
  • Dillad (clothes)
  • Oer (cold)
  • Glaw (rain)

Pick two or three. That is enough.

Step 2: Get Ready (optional)

Mini practice: Point at an item and name it.

  • Tywel
  • Esgidiau
  • Dillad

No pressure. This is just pattern building.

Step 3: Live session

  • Kit-based language discussion
  • Mentimeter: what do you need for a cold dip or walk?
  • Simple weather words in context

You can keep it playful and practical.

Step 4: After the session

Small follow-on: Notice and name one item or one weather word in Welsh during the week.

Even thinking it quietly counts.

Step 6: Curiosity thread (optional)

Choose one:

  • Look up what a local Welsh place name means.
  • Notice Welsh words used outdoors.
  • Bring one outdoor word you like to the next session.

This is a stretch option. It is for interest and conversation, not performance.
