Session 2: Basic introductions and interactions

Session aim: Get comfortable introducing yourself and handling simple, friendly interactions.

How we will achieve this: Short listening practice, repeat-after-me phrases, and low-stakes pair activities in the live session.

What we will cover: Saying your name, where you are from, and simple “nice to meet you” style responses.

Outcome: You will be able to introduce yourself in a simple way and understand the basic shape of a friendly Welsh greeting.

How to use this page: Use the arrows or step buttons below. If your time or energy is low, do Get Ready only.

Step 1: Get Ready

Listen to the short audio or video your facilitator shares and notice the rhythm of introductions. No memorising needed.

Step 2: More Get Ready

Pick one phrase you like. Write it down or save it in your notes. That is your “good enough” prep.

Step 3: Live session

You will practise introductions as a group, then try tiny pair activities where you can speak as much or as little as you like.

Step 4: After the session

Send one short intro-style message to the WhatsApp group. Even “shwmae” and your name is a win.

Step 5: Curiosity thread

Find one Welsh word used locally (signs, place names, cafés). Bring it back to share, even if you are not sure how to say it yet.
