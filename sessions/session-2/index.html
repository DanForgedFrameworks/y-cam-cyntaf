Session 2: Simple introductions and interactions

How this session works

Rhythm: Digital pre-work → Live session → Light homework or social application

This is designed to feel low pressure. Small steps count.

Aim: Feel more confident introducing yourself and having a tiny “hello” interaction.

How we’ll achieve this: Repeat-and-build practice, friendly prompts, and short role-play that you can opt out of.

What we’ll cover: Name, where you are from / where you live, simple questions and answers.

Outcome: You can do a short intro without overthinking it.

How to use this page: Use the arrows to move through the steps. Low time or low energy? Do Get Ready and you will still be ready enough.

Step 1: Get Ready

Try these once:

  • Dw i’n … (I’m …)
  • Dw i’n byw yn … (I live in …)
  • Sut wyt ti? (How are you?)

Pick one phrase that feels doable.

Step 2: Get Ready (optional)

Mini practice: Say your intro in three chunks.

  • Shwmae
  • Dw i’n …
  • Dw i’n byw yn …

Step 3: Live session

  • Warm-up greetings
  • Simple “ask and answer” patterns
  • Gentle pair practice with prompts (no pressure)

Step 4: After the session

Small follow-on: Write a one-line intro in Welsh (even if you do not send it to anyone).

This is confidence training, not a test.

Step 6: Curiosity thread (optional)

Choose one:

  • Find a Welsh phrase for “I’m tired” or “I’m alright” and bring it next time.
  • Look up a Welsh place name and what it means.
  • Notice one Welsh sign in your area and take a photo.
